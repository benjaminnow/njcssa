History



  • 2015

    Newark Kids Code is founded

    The Urban League of Essex County establishes Newark Kids Code. The mission of Newark Kids Code is to create the next generation of software engineers, and use coding as a tool to teach all students to be computational thinkers, positioning them for sustained professional success in a modern economic landscape.

  • 2015-2018

    Newark Kids Code runs several coding camps and workshops with the help of NJIT students.

    Newark Kids Code, in partnership with NJIT, runs 3 coding camps. They graduated 3 classes of students which are taught in the Scratch programming language. As you can see in the pictures when you click on the circle, kids were having a lot of fun at this camp. Based on the success of Newark Kids Code, Mr. Sharif started to plan for ways of expanding CS opportunities.

  • 2015

    Tech House Purchase

    The Urban League of Essex County buys a Victorian house in West Newark. The plan is to make the Tech House into a retreat for CS students and teachers and provide a pipeline of talented workers to tech companies in Newark and beyond. Funding for the Tech House project is currently under way.

  • September 2018

    NJCSSA is founded.

    The NJCSSA is founded to recruit skilled peer instructors. In partnership with Newark Kids Code, the NJCSSA will offer coding programs to students from Newark high schools. Successful results will lead to partnerships with large tech companies and will provide the necessary money to get the Tech House project off the ground.

Initially, the NJCSSA will be working out of a building owned by the Urban League of Essex County. Each student entering the program will be provided with a laptop on loan. Classes will be held on several weekdays after school and on weekends for 10 week sessions. The purpose of this temporary setup is to demonstrate our potential to corporate sponsors.

Long-term Goals



Corporate Partnerships

The NJCSSA project will operate out of the Urban League of Essex County's office in Newark until renovations on the Tech House are completed. Organizations such as Google and Panasonic have expressed interest in this project. The success of the NJCSSA pilot at the Urban League's office, along with ongoing Newark Kids Code programs, will support fundraising efforts from corporate sponsors.

Tech-House

  • A well-equipped Tech space for kids in Newark, owned by the Urban League of Essex County
  • Conveniently located next to NJIT and Rutgers Newark campuses
  • Flexible operating hours
  • No tuition or fees for students
  • A pipeline for skilled talent to flow into Newark
